Where Orange County Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Orange County is transported to the the city police department jail where the arrest happened at the nearest Orange County city police department jail (phone: 626-478-1062). Orange County has roughly 30 city police departments, plus the Orange County Sheriff's Department. A working OC bail bondsman knows every city lockup — from Huntington Beach PD to Newport Beach PD to Tustin PD — as well as the main county intake facility at 550 North Flower.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703. This is the intake point for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD) jail system, which also includes the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. Angels Bail Bonds posts bonds at every one of these facilities.

Orange County in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Orange County processes thousands of arrests a month through the Orange County Sheriff's Department jail system. The main intake facility is the Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street in Santa Ana, with long-term holding at the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and minimum-security overflow at the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. An Orange County bail bondsman works all three facilities continuously.

The OC bail schedule is set by the Superior Court of California, County of Orange, and runs independent from LA and San Diego county schedules. Representative amounts: DUI first-offense $15,000 (VC 23152), DUI with injury $100,000 (VC 23153), domestic violence corporal injury $50,000 (PC 273.5), drug possession for sale $30,000 (HS 11351/11378), assault with deadly weapon $30,000 (PC 245(a)(1)). These are assigned at booking and apply unless a judge modifies them at arraignment.

Orange County's 34 cities include a mix of city-PD jurisdictions (Huntington Beach, Newport Beach, Santa Ana, Anaheim, Irvine, Garden Grove, Fullerton, and others) and OCSD contract cities (Aliso Viejo, Dana Point, Laguna Hills, Laguna Niguel, Lake Forest, Mission Viejo, Rancho Santa Margarita, Stanton, Villa Park, Yorba Linda, and more). The practical difference: a city-PD arrest can often release via bond at the city jail. An OCSD arrest always routes through the IRC, with a longer processing time. Angels Bail Bonds knows every city jail and every OCSD substation in the county.

Typical Late-Night Arrest Scenarios in Orange County

A first-offense DUI on the 5 freeway in South OC — bail scheduled at $15,000, 10% fee = $1,500. If the arresting city has a city jail (Mission Viejo is OCSD contract, San Clemente has no city jail either), booking goes to OCSD and transport to IRC.

A felony domestic violence arrest in Huntington Beach — PC 273.5 bail scheduled at $50,000. HB has its own city jail at 2000 Main Street; bond can be posted there before transfer to IRC or Theo Lacy.

An assault with deadly weapon arrest in Anaheim — PC 245(a)(1) bail scheduled at $30,000. APD city jail processes the booking; felony holds typically transfer to Theo Lacy within 4–6 hours.

How a Orange County Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Orange County bail schedule — orange county bail bondsman typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at OC city PD jail and 4–8 hours at the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Orange County arrest actually get processed?

An Orange County bail bondsman posts bonds at every city jail, OCSD substation, and the Orange County IRC at 550 N Flower Street in Santa Ana. The OC bail schedule is separate from LA and San Diego schedules. Angels Bail Bonds has been licensed in OC since 1958.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Orange County?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Orange County bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Orange County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Orange County DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
